Installing on Mac

One quick "yes" and you're in.

Etaros isn't in the App Store yet, so macOS asks you to confirm once. Totally safe — here's exactly what to do.

1

Open & try to launch

Open the .dmg, drag Etaros to Applications, then double-click it. macOS shows a "not opened" popup — that's expected. Click OK.

2

Settings → Privacy & Security

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down. You'll see "Etaros was blocked" → click Open Anyway.

3

Confirm with Touch ID

Touch ID or your password, then click Open. macOS remembers it — you only do this once. Done!

🔒 Why the warning? Apps need a paid Apple certificate to skip this step. Etaros is new and independent — this step is normal for indie apps and completely safe. On newer macOS (Sequoia+) you confirm via Settings instead of right-click. Same on Apple Silicon (M1–M4) and Intel.
